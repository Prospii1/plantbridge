# THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol)

THC is the primary psychoactive compound in cannabis. It is responsible for the intoxicating effects most associated with cannabis use and is also the subject of a large body of wellness research.

## How THC Works

THC binds directly to CB1 receptors in the brain and nervous system, producing its characteristic effects. CB1 receptors are concentrated in areas associated with memory, coordination, time perception, and mood — which explains the range of effects THC can produce.

THC also binds to CB2 receptors found throughout the immune system, which is an area of active research interest.

## Potential Wellness Associations

Research suggests THC may be associated with:

- **Relaxation** — Many users report a sense of physical and mental ease; this is among the most commonly cited reasons for adult-use cannabis
- **Sleep support** — THC may help with sleep onset, though research suggests heavy use over time can affect sleep architecture
- **Appetite support** — THC is well-established as an appetite stimulant; this is why THC-based medications (like dronabinol) are used in clinical settings for specific conditions
- **Comfort** — Users often explore cannabis for physical comfort; evidence varies by type and delivery method

## Understanding Dose

Dose is perhaps the most important variable with THC. Effects can be highly dose-dependent:

- Low doses tend to be associated with relaxation, mild mood lift, and enhanced sensory perception
- Higher doses increase the likelihood of uncomfortable effects including anxiety, paranoia, or disorientation

The principle "start low, go slow" is widely recommended, especially for new users or those returning after a break.

## Formats and Onset Times

| Format | Onset | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Inhaled (flower/vape) | 2–10 min | 1–3 hrs | Fast onset makes titration easier |
| Sublingual (tincture) | 15–45 min | 2–4 hrs | More predictable than edibles |
| Edibles | 45–120 min | 4–8 hrs | Easy to over-consume; wait before re-dosing |
| Topicals | Variable | Local | Minimal psychoactive effect |

## THC:CBD Ratio

Many experienced users pay close attention to the ratio of THC to CBD in a product. CBD may moderate some of the intensity of THC's psychoactive effects. Products with a balanced THC:CBD ratio (e.g., 1:1) are often explored by people who want the potential benefits of both cannabinoids with a gentler experience.

## Tolerance and Breaks

Regular THC use can lead to tolerance — requiring more to achieve the same effect. Periodic tolerance breaks (sometimes called "T-breaks") are commonly practiced by regular users to reset sensitivity.

## Drug Interactions and Considerations

THC interacts with the central nervous system and may amplify the effects of other depressants including alcohol, benzodiazepines, and opioids. It is also metabolized by cytochrome P450 enzymes.

> **Important:** Talk to your healthcare provider before using THC if you take prescription medications, have a personal or family history of psychosis or substance use disorder, or are pregnant or breastfeeding. THC impairs driving and operating machinery — never drive under the influence of cannabis.
