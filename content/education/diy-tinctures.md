# Making Cannabis Tinctures at Home

Tinctures are one of the most popular home preparations — they offer precise dosing, long shelf life, and fast absorption when taken sublingually (under the tongue). This guide covers the two main methods: alcohol extraction and oil infusion.

## Method 1: Alcohol Tincture (Most Efficient)

Alcohol is an effective solvent that extracts a broad range of cannabinoids and terpenes.

**What you need:**
- Decarboxylated cannabis material (see [DIY Basics](/education/diy-cannabis-basics))
- High-proof grain alcohol (190 proof / 95% ethanol)
- Glass jar with tight-fitting lid
- Fine mesh strainer + cheesecloth
- Dark glass dropper bottles

**Process (quick wash method):**
1. Place decarbed cannabis in a glass jar
2. Pour just enough alcohol to cover (cold alcohol extracts more cleanly — freeze the jar and alcohol for 30 min first)
3. Shake vigorously for 1–3 minutes
4. Immediately strain through cheesecloth, pressing firmly
5. Transfer to dark dropper bottles; label with date and estimated concentration
6. Store in a cool, dark place — good for 1–2 years

**Why the quick wash?** Longer soaks extract more chlorophyll (green colour, bitter taste) along with cannabinoids. A quick wash gets cannabinoids with less plant matter.

## Method 2: Oil Infusion (No Alcohol)

For those who prefer not to use alcohol, oil infusions work well for sublingual use or cooking.

**Best carrier oils:**
- **MCT (fractionated coconut) oil** — light, neutral, absorbs quickly
- **Olive oil** — widely available, slight flavour
- **Hemp seed oil** — complementary nutrients but shorter shelf life

**Process (slow cooker method):**
1. Combine decarbed cannabis and carrier oil (1–2 oz cannabis per 16 oz oil is a common ratio)
2. Cook on lowest setting (160–180°F / 70–80°C) for 2–4 hours
3. Stir occasionally; do not exceed 200°F to preserve cannabinoids
4. Strain through cheesecloth, squeezing to capture all oil
5. Store in dark glass; refrigerate for up to 3 months

## Estimating Potency

Exact potency is hard to calculate at home. A rough estimate:
- If using hemp flower with 15% CBD, 1g flower ≈ 150 mg CBD
- With ~70% extraction efficiency, you'd get ~105 mg CBD per gram
- In 30 mL of oil, that's roughly 3.5 mg CBD per mL

Always start with the lowest dose (1–2 drops) and increase slowly over several days.

## Administration

- **Sublingual** — place under tongue, hold 60 seconds, then swallow
- **Added to food or drink** — slower onset (60–90 min), longer duration
- **Directly swallowed** — digested like an edible

> **Educational note:** Home preparation potency varies significantly. This information is for educational purposes only. Verify local laws before making cannabis preparations.
