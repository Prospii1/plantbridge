# Linalool

Linalool is a floral-scented terpene found naturally in lavender, birch trees, coriander, and many cannabis varieties. It is one of the most studied terpenes due to its long history of use in aromatherapy and herbal wellness traditions.

## Aroma Profile

Linalool has a distinctly floral, slightly spicy scent — most recognizable as the characteristic aroma of lavender. In cannabis, it contributes a soft, calming fragrance that many users find pleasant.

## What Research Suggests

Linalool has been studied for a range of potential properties:

- **Relaxation support** — Preclinical research and human aromatherapy studies suggest linalool may support a sense of calm. Some studies have explored its interaction with serotonin pathways.
- **Sleep support** — Linalool-rich products are frequently explored for evening use. Research in animal models suggests potential sedative-adjacent properties, though human studies are limited.
- **Stress response** — Some studies have looked at linalool's effect on stress markers, with promising early results.

The mechanism appears to involve interactions with the GABAergic system — the same pathway targeted by many anti-anxiety medications — though the degree of effect from inhaled or ingested linalool in realistic doses remains an open research question.

## Common Linalool-Rich Cannabis Varieties

Strains and cultivars with notable linalool content tend to be marketed under names evoking lavender, floral, or relaxing themes. Always check a product's certificate of analysis (COA) for actual terpene percentages rather than relying on marketing names.

## The Entourage Effect

Linalool is thought to work synergistically with cannabinoids. Some research suggests it may complement CBD's calming properties and may soften the intensity of THC's psychoactive effects.

## Everyday Sources

You likely already encounter linalool regularly — it is widely used in:
- Lavender essential oils and aromatherapy products
- Many cleaning products and personal care items
- Herbal teas (lavender, chamomile blends)
- Certain spices like coriander and cinnamon

## A Note on Evidence

Most linalool research has been conducted in vitro (cell studies) or in animal models. Human clinical trials are limited. The wellness associations described here are based on emerging research and traditional use patterns, not established medical claims.
