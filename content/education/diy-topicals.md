# Making Cannabis Topicals at Home

Cannabis-infused topicals — creams, balms, and salves — are applied directly to the skin for localized relief. They generally do not produce psychoactive effects because cannabinoids penetrate only the top layers of skin rather than reaching the bloodstream in significant amounts.

## Why Topicals?

- **Targeted application** — apply exactly where needed
- **Non-psychoactive** — generally safe for all-day use
- **Complementary** — can be combined with internal preparations
- Widely explored for joint discomfort, muscle soreness, and skin conditions

## Basic Cannabis Salve

A salve is one of the simplest topicals — just infused oil solidified with beeswax.

**What you need:**
- 1 cup cannabis-infused oil (see [Tinctures guide](/education/diy-tinctures) for oil infusion method)
- 1 oz beeswax (or more for firmer consistency)
- Optional: 10–20 drops essential oil (lavender, peppermint, or eucalyptus)
- Small glass jars or tins for storage

**Process:**
1. Melt beeswax in a double boiler or heatproof bowl over simmering water
2. Add infused oil and stir until fully combined
3. Remove from heat; let cool slightly (about 1 minute)
4. Add essential oils if using
5. Pour into clean containers and let set — do not disturb until fully solid (1–2 hours)
6. Label with contents, date, and "For topical use only"

**Ratio guide:**
- 1 oz beeswax to 1 cup oil = firm salve (sticks, balm consistency)
- ½ oz beeswax to 1 cup oil = soft salve (lotion-like when warm)

## Enhancing Your Topical

Consider adding complementary botanicals:
- **Arnica** — traditionally used for muscle discomfort and bruising
- **Cayenne (capsaicin)** — warming effect; may enhance permeability
- **Menthol or peppermint** — cooling sensation
- **Lavender essential oil** — adds linalool (calming terpene) and pleasant scent

## Water-Based Lotion

For a lighter, more easily absorbed product, you can make an emulsion (oil + water). This requires an emulsifier (e.g., BTMS or emulsifying wax) and more care to avoid contamination. Start with a basic salve before attempting emulsions.

## Storage and Safety

- Topicals without water have a long shelf life (6–12 months in a cool, dark place)
- Water-based products require a preservative and refrigeration
- Always label clearly with date and contents
- Patch test before first use
- Keep away from eyes, mucous membranes, and broken skin
