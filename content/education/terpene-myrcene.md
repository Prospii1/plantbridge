# Myrcene

Myrcene is the most abundant terpene found in most cannabis varieties. It has an earthy, musky, herbal aroma with subtle fruity notes sometimes described as mango-like. Myrcene is also found in hops (the plant that gives beer its bitterness), mango, thyme, and lemongrass.

## Aroma Profile

Myrcene's scent is often described as:
- Earthy and musky (similar to damp soil or moss)
- Herbal and slightly spicy
- With a ripe, tropical fruit undertone in some profiles

Products high in myrcene tend to have a grounding, natural aroma rather than a bright or citrusy one.

## What Research Suggests

Myrcene is one of the most studied cannabis terpenes:

- **Physical relaxation** — Myrcene has been associated with sedative-like properties in preclinical studies. High-myrcene cannabis is often explored by users seeking body-focused relaxation.
- **Comfort support** — Some research suggests myrcene may have analgesic-adjacent properties, potentially interacting with opioid receptors in animal models — though this is far from established in humans.
- **The entourage effect** — Myrcene is thought to enhance the permeability of cellular membranes, which some researchers suggest may allow cannabinoids to cross the blood-brain barrier more readily. This remains a hypothesis under investigation.

## The "Mango Trick"

A popular folk belief holds that eating mango before consuming cannabis enhances effects, due to mango's myrcene content. While the idea is plausible given myrcene's proposed membrane effects, direct evidence in humans is limited.

## Myrcene and "Indica" Effects

The traditional indica/sativa classification system is not botanically rigorous — modern cannabis is highly hybridized. However, many cultivars historically marketed as "indica" tend to be high in myrcene, which may partly explain the relaxing, body-heavy effects often associated with that category.

## Common Sources Beyond Cannabis

- **Hops** — Craft beer drinkers may notice that some hop-forward styles share aromatic notes with high-myrcene cannabis
- **Mango** — Ripe mangoes contain notable myrcene concentrations
- **Lemongrass and thyme** — Common culinary herbs with myrcene present

## Checking Terpene Content

When reviewing a cannabis product's certificate of analysis (COA), myrcene is typically listed as a percentage of total weight. Products with myrcene above 0.5% are generally considered myrcene-dominant.
