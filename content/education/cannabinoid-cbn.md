# CBN (Cannabinol)

CBN is a mildly psychoactive cannabinoid that forms naturally as THC ages and oxidizes. It is found in small quantities in fresh cannabis and in higher concentrations in older or improperly stored material. Unlike THC, CBN's psychoactive effect is very mild — roughly 10% of THC's potency by most estimates.

## How CBN Interacts with the Body

CBN binds to both CB1 and CB2 receptors, with a relatively stronger affinity for CB2 than THC has. It also interacts with TRPV2 receptors involved in pain signaling. Researchers are exploring its relationship with the sleep-wake cycle, though the mechanism is not fully understood.

## What Research Suggests

CBN's reputation as a sleep aid comes primarily from a 1975 study and widespread anecdotal reports. More recent, rigorous research is limited:

- **Sleep support** — Many users report that CBN helps them stay asleep longer; formal clinical evidence is still emerging
- **Sedation when combined** — Some researchers suggest the sedating effect attributed to CBN may actually come from the terpenes present alongside it (particularly myrcene) rather than CBN alone
- **Discomfort** — Early preclinical studies suggest potential for supporting comfort, particularly in combination with CBD
- **Appetite** — Some research suggests CBN may influence appetite, though less potently than THC

## When CBN May Be Most Relevant

CBN is most often explored for:
- Difficulty staying asleep (sleep maintenance rather than sleep onset)
- Evening use as a wind-down support
- Older adults seeking a gentle, non-intoxicating option

## Formats

- **Tinctures and oils** — most common; look for products tested for CBN specifically
- **Capsules/softgels** — popular for sleep routines due to timed release
- **Gummies** — widely marketed as sleep formulas; verify CBN content on COA

## Starting Guidance

Educational starting points: 2.5–5 mg for new users, 15–25 mg for experienced. Take 30–60 minutes before bed.
