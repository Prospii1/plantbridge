# Understanding Terpenes

Terpenes are aromatic compounds found in many plants — including cannabis, lavender, citrus fruits, and pine trees. They are responsible for the distinctive smells of different cannabis varieties and, according to emerging research, may play a meaningful role in the overall wellness experience.

## Why Terpenes Matter

Beyond aroma, terpenes may interact with cannabinoids and with the body's own systems to shape the character of a cannabis experience. This interaction — sometimes called the entourage effect — suggests that the full chemical profile of a plant matters, not just its THC or CBD content.

Terpenes act on various receptors in the body, including serotonin and dopamine pathways, though research into their specific effects in humans is still developing.

## How to Think About Terpenes

When exploring cannabis products, terpene content is often listed on lab-tested product packaging (certificates of analysis, or COAs). Many dispensaries also organize their inventory by dominant terpene profiles rather than just the traditional indica/sativa/hybrid categories — a more chemically meaningful way to understand potential experiences.

## Common Terpenes in Cannabis

| Terpene | Aroma | Also found in |
|---|---|---|
| Myrcene | Earthy, musky, herbal | Hops, mango, thyme |
| Linalool | Floral, lavender | Lavender, birch, coriander |
| Limonene | Citrus, bright | Citrus peels, juniper |
| Caryophyllene | Spicy, peppery | Black pepper, cloves, rosemary |
| Pinene | Fresh pine | Pine needles, rosemary, basil |
| Terpinolene | Fresh, floral, herbal | Nutmeg, tea tree, lilac |
| Humulene | Earthy, woody, hoppy | Hops, sage, ginseng |

## Choosing by Terpene Profile

Rather than picking cannabis by strain name alone (which is not standardized across the industry), experienced users often look for consistent terpene profiles. If a particular terpene-forward product supported a wellness goal well, looking for similar terpene content in future products can be a useful approach.

## A Note on Research

Terpene research is active but still maturing. Many studies are preclinical (conducted in cells or animals) or rely on self-reported user data. The experiences people associate with different terpene profiles are real, but the precise mechanisms are not yet fully understood.

> **Educational note:** This content is for informational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Individual responses to terpenes vary. Consult your healthcare provider before making changes to your wellness routine.
