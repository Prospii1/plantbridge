# DIY Cannabis Medicine: An Introduction

Many people choose to prepare their own cannabis-infused products at home for greater control over ingredients, potency, and cost. This guide covers the basics of safe home preparation — what's involved, the key steps, and important considerations before you start.

## Why Make Your Own?

- **Control over ingredients** — You know exactly what goes in
- **Cost efficiency** — Home preparation can significantly reduce per-dose cost
- **Customization** — Adjust cannabinoid ratios, carrier oils, and concentrations
- **Accessibility** — Useful when specific commercial products aren't available locally

## The Core Process: Decarboxylation

Before cannabis can be used in most preparations, it must be **decarboxylated** — heated to convert THCA and CBDA (inactive acid forms) into THC and CBD (active forms). Without this step, the preparation will have minimal effect.

**Basic decarboxylation:**
1. Preheat oven to 240°F (115°C)
2. Break cannabis material into small pieces (don't grind too finely)
3. Spread on a parchment-lined baking sheet
4. Bake 40–60 minutes, stirring every 15 minutes
5. Material should look lightly toasted and smell nutty

> **Note:** Temperature matters. Below 220°F, decarboxylation is incomplete. Above 300°F, terpenes and cannabinoids begin to degrade.

## High-CBD Starting Material

For wellness-focused preparations, high-CBD hemp flower is widely available and legal in most US states. It provides all the non-psychoactive benefits without significant THC. When sourcing, look for:

- Certificate of Analysis (COA) from an accredited lab
- CBD percentage and THC percentage clearly listed
- Pesticide and heavy metal testing results

## Safety and Legal Considerations

- Check your state's laws regarding home cannabis preparation
- Store preparations securely away from children and pets
- Label all preparations clearly with contents and date made
- Start with low doses and increase gradually — home preparations vary in potency
- Do not use cooking equipment for cannabis preparation and food preparation without thorough cleaning
