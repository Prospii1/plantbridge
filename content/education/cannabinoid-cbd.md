# CBD (Cannabidiol)

CBD is one of the most abundant cannabinoids in the cannabis plant and has attracted more scientific attention than almost any other phytocannabinoid. Unlike THC, CBD is non-intoxicating — it does not produce a "high."

## How CBD Interacts with the Body

CBD's mechanism of action is more complex than THC's. Rather than binding strongly to CB1 or CB2 receptors, CBD interacts with a broader range of targets including serotonin receptors (5-HT1A), TRPV1 receptors involved in pain and inflammation signaling, and GPR55 receptors. It may also modulate how the endocannabinoid system processes its own naturally occurring molecules.

## What Research Suggests

Research suggests CBD may support:

- **A sense of calm** — Several studies and a substantial body of user reports suggest CBD may support relaxation and reduced feelings of everyday stress
- **Sleep quality** — Some users explore CBD as part of an evening wind-down routine; research is ongoing on its relationship to sleep architecture
- **General comfort** — Topical CBD products are widely used by people exploring discomfort relief; evidence is mixed but interest is growing

The FDA has approved one CBD-based medication (Epidiolex) for specific, rare seizure conditions. This approval is not applicable to over-the-counter CBD wellness products, which are held to different regulatory standards.

## Formats and Considerations

CBD is available in many forms, each with different absorption profiles:

- **Tinctures/oils** — absorbed under the tongue; onset 15–45 minutes
- **Capsules/edibles** — pass through digestion; onset 45–90 minutes, longer duration
- **Topicals** — applied to skin; local effect, minimal systemic absorption
- **Vaporized flower or concentrate** — fastest onset (minutes), shortest duration

## Full-Spectrum vs. Broad-Spectrum vs. Isolate

- **Full-spectrum CBD** contains CBD alongside other cannabinoids (including trace THC below 0.3%), terpenes, and plant compounds
- **Broad-spectrum CBD** has most compounds but THC removed
- **CBD isolate** is pure CBD with nothing else

Research suggests full-spectrum products may offer greater benefit through the entourage effect, though isolates are preferred by those who need to avoid any THC.

## Drug Interactions

CBD is metabolized by the cytochrome P450 enzyme family — the same pathway used by many prescription medications. This means CBD can potentially affect how other drugs are processed in your body.

> **Important:** Talk to your healthcare provider before using CBD if you take any prescription medications. This is especially relevant for blood thinners, anti-epileptics, and certain antidepressants.
